Calories for Weight Loss

Weight loss happens when your body burns more calories than it consumes. This is known as a calorie deficit. Understanding how to calculate and manage this deficit is the key to sustainable fat loss.

What is a calorie deficit?

A calorie deficit means eating fewer calories than your Total Daily Energy Expenditure (TDEE). When maintained consistently, your body uses stored fat as energy.

How big should the deficit be?

  • Small deficit (200–300 kcal): slow but sustainable fat loss
  • Moderate deficit (400–500 kcal): balanced and effective for most people
  • Large deficit (700+ kcal): faster loss but harder to maintain

Common mistakes

  • Cutting calories too aggressively
  • Ignoring protein intake
  • Not accounting for activity level
  • Expecting rapid results
