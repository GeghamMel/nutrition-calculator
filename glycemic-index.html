What is the Glycemic Index?

1

How GI is measured

GI measures how quickly a carbohydrate-containing food raises blood glucose compared to pure glucose (GI = 100). Foods are tested on real people over 2 hours after a standard 50g carbohydrate portion.

2

Why GI matters

High-GI foods cause rapid blood sugar spikes followed by crashes, triggering hunger and energy dips. Low-GI foods provide steadier energy, better appetite control, and are associated with lower diabetes risk.

3

GI vs Glycemic Load

GI doesn't account for portion size. Glycemic Load (GL) = GI × carbs per serving / 100. Watermelon has a high GI (72) but low GL per typical serving because it's mostly water — the actual blood sugar impact is small.
