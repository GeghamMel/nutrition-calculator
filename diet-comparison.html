Diet Comparison Guide

Compare the most popular diets — macros, evidence, pros, cons, and who each diet suits best.

With dozens of popular eating patterns competing for attention, the honest answer is that no single diet is objectively 'best' — research consistently shows that the diet you can actually stick to long-term outperforms a theoretically optimal one you abandon after three weeks. What varies between diets is the macro split, the level of restriction, the evidence base behind specific health claims, and how well each pattern fits different lifestyles, food preferences, and goals. This comparison lays out the macro breakdown, strength of evidence, and who each approach tends to suit, so you can make an informed choice rather than following whichever diet is trending.

Where diets genuinely differ is in adherence (how easy each is to sustain for a given person), specific health markers beyond weight, and food quality, not some unique 'metabolic advantage' of one approach over another.
Consider your food preferences, lifestyle, any medical conditions, and how restrictive an approach you can realistically sustain for months, not weeks. A diet that requires eliminating entire food groups you enjoy is statistically less likely to be maintained long-term than a more moderate approach, even if the stricter one theoretically produces faster short-term results.
