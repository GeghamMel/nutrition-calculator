About metabolism

Why metabolism slows with age

1

Muscle loss (sarcopenia)

After 30, adults lose 3–8% of muscle per decade without resistance training, reducing BMR.

2

Hormonal changes

Declining sex hormones reduce anabolic drive, making muscle maintenance harder with age.

3

Reduced physical activity

NEAT decreases with age — less spontaneous movement reduces daily calorie burn by 200–400 kcal.

4

How to fight back

Resistance training, high protein, staying active, and quality sleep are the best counters.

Methodology

How BMR and TDEE are calculated

This calculator uses the Mifflin-St Jeor equation, published in 1990, which is generally considered the most accurate formula available for estimating Basal Metabolic Rate (BMR) — the calories your body burns at complete rest just to keep vital functions running (breathing, circulation, cell repair). Independent validation studies have found it more accurate than older formulas like Harris-Benedict for most modern populations. Your Total Daily Energy Expenditure (TDEE) is then calculated by multiplying BMR by an activity multiplier that accounts for exercise and daily movement.

Even the best formulas carry a margin of error of around 10% for the general population, since BMR is also influenced by genetics, muscle mass, thyroid function, and other factors that a simple age/weight/height formula can't capture. Use the result as a starting point: track your actual weight trend against your intake for 2-3 weeks and adjust the number up or down by 100-150 kcal based on real-world results rather than treating the calculator's output as exact.
