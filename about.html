About Calories & Macros

A free, science-based nutrition calculator built for people who want clear, honest estimates — without paywalls or required sign-ups.

What this tool does

Calories & Macros calculates your estimated daily calorie needs based on your age, height, weight, sex, activity level, and goal. It then splits those calories into protein, carbohydrates, and fat — your three macronutrients — using goal-specific ratios backed by nutrition research.

Whether you want to lose fat, build muscle, or simply understand how much you should be eating, the calculator gives you a clear starting point in under a minute.

The formulas we use

We use the Mifflin-St Jeor equation to calculate Basal Metabolic Rate (BMR). This formula was validated in a 1990 study and is consistently ranked as one of the most accurate BMR predictors for healthy adults by nutritional science bodies including the Academy of Nutrition and Dietetics.

Your BMR is then multiplied by an activity factor (also known as the Harris-Benedict activity multiplier) to estimate your Total Daily Energy Expenditure (TDEE). Calorie targets for fat loss and muscle gain are then derived by applying evidence-based deficit and surplus percentages to your TDEE.

Why we built it

Many nutrition calculators online are cluttered with ads, hidden behind email forms, or produce results without explaining the methodology. We wanted to build something different: a fast, transparent tool that treats users as intelligent adults who deserve to understand how their numbers are calculated.

Who this is for

  • People starting a fat loss or muscle gain phase who need a calorie and macro target
  • Athletes and gym-goers who want to cross-check their current intake
  • Beginners learning about nutrition who want a clear, educational starting point
  • Anyone curious about their TDEE and how activity affects calorie needs

Important disclaimer

The results produced by this calculator are estimates based on population-average formulas. Individual metabolism varies due to factors including muscle mass, hormonal status, gut microbiome, and genetics. The information provided here is for educational purposes only and does not constitute medical or nutritional advice.

If you have a health condition — including but not limited to diabetes, kidney disease, thyroid disorders, eating disorder history, or cardiovascular disease — please consult a qualified healthcare professional or registered dietitian before changing your diet.
