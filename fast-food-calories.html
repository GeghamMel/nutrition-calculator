Fast Food Calories

Complete calorie table for McDonald's, KFC, Burger King, Subway, Pizza Hut and more — with protein, carbs and fat per item.

Fast food doesn't have to derail a calorie or macro goal — it just requires knowing what you're actually ordering. Menu items at the same restaurant can vary by 300-500+ calories depending on size and toppings, and 'healthy-sounding' items like salads with dressing or grilled chicken wraps sometimes carry more calories than a basic burger. This table pulls calorie, protein, carb, and fat data directly from official nutrition information published by each restaurant chain, so you can check before you order rather than guess.

FAQ

Frequently asked questions about fast food calories

Dressings, cheese, croutons, and creamy toppings can add 200-400+ calories on top of the base salad, sometimes exceeding a simple burger. A salad with a light vinaigrette is genuinely lower calorie, but a salad with ranch dressing and fried chicken can easily out-calorie a plain burger — the word 'salad' alone doesn't guarantee a low-calorie choice.
Generally reliable for standardised chain items, since large chains use consistent portioning and recipes tested in labs, and are legally required in many countries to publish accurate figures. That said, in-store variation in portion size (a slightly heavier scoop, extra sauce) can shift actual calories by 5-15% from the published number in either direction.
Skipping or downsizing sugary drinks and fries tends to have the biggest impact, since a large soda alone can be 300-400 calories of essentially just sugar, and fries add another 300-500 depending on size — often more than the sandwich or burger itself. Swapping a large drink for water or a diet option is usually the single easiest calorie cut available.

Checking the calorie count of your usual order and fitting it into your daily target occasionally is generally more sustainable long-term than trying to avoid fast food entirely, which often backfires for many people.
